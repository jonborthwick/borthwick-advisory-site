Context
You're running a subscription gaming bundle — think Humble Choice or Xbox Game Pass Indie tier — and the question is really about which growth levers matter most at 3,000 subscribers before you burn money on the wrong ones.
The Answer

At 3,000 subs and $5/month, you're at ~$180K ARR. Doubling means getting to 6,000 subs or finding ways to expand revenue per subscriber — ideally both. Here's how I'd prioritize:

1. Fix retention before pouring water into a leaky bucket
Before any acquisition spend, know your monthly churn rate. At this scale, even 8% monthly churn means you're replacing nearly your entire subscriber base every year just to stay flat. Target under 5% monthly.
2. Add a second price point — annual plans
Offer an annual plan at $45–48/year (20–25% discount). This drops monthly churn to near zero for that cohort, pulls cash forward, and signals commitment from your best customers.
3. Launch a referral loop
Your existing 3,000 subs are your cheapest acquisition channel. Give subscribers a unique link — one free month for them, first month free for the referred friend. A 10% referral conversion rate generates 300 new subs with near-zero CAC.
Priority Order for Next 90 Days
1. Know your churn rate  ·  2. Launch annual plans  ·  3. Launch referral program  ·  4. Test one distribution partnership
One Thing to Pressure-Test
Do you have licensing rights that allow you to bundle and distribute these 100+ games in a subscription model, and are those rights exclusive enough to defend against a larger player replicating your catalog?
