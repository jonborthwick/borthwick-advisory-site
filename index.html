We sell indie games — over 100 titles, 3,000 subscribers, $5/month.
The Question
How can we double size in the next 12 months?
Advisory Response
Copy New question
Context
You're running a subscription gaming bundle — think Humble Choice or Xbox Game Pass Indie tier — and the question is really about which growth levers matter most at 3,000 subscribers before you burn money on the wrong ones.
The Answer

At 3,000 subs and $5/month, you're at ~$180K ARR. Doubling means getting to 6,000 subs or finding ways to expand revenue per subscriber — ideally both. Here's how I'd prioritize:

1. Fix retention before pouring water into a leaky bucket
Before any acquisition spend, know your monthly churn rate. At this scale, even 8% monthly churn means you're replacing nearly your entire subscriber base every year just to stay flat. Target under 5% monthly. Retention is your growth multiplier — every point you save compounds.
2. Add a second price point — annual plans
Offer an annual plan at $45–48/year (20–25% discount). This does three things: drops monthly churn to near zero for that cohort, pulls cash forward, and signals commitment from your best customers. Even if only 20% of new subs take annual, your blended churn profile improves materially. This is the single highest-ROI pricing move at your stage.
3. Launch a referral loop
Your existing 3,000 subs are your cheapest acquisition channel and you're almost certainly not using them. Give subscribers a unique link — one free month for them, first month free or discounted for the referred friend. Gaming audiences are tribal. A 10% referral conversion rate on your base generates 300 new subs with near-zero CAC.
Priority Order for Next 90 Days
1. Know your churn rate  ·  2. Launch annual plans  ·  3. Launch referral program  ·  4. Test one distribution partnership
One Thing to Pressure-Test
Do you have licensing rights that allow you to bundle and distribute these 100+ games in a subscription model, and are those rights exclusive enough to defend against a larger player replicating your catalog? Your catalog relationships are your moat; make sure they're contractually solid before you scale acquisition aggressively.

Connected in Action

Five questions that change when
the advisor has live data.

These are the kinds of questions Connected answers in seconds — not because the AI is smarter, but because it's reading from your actual Salesforce records, not a hypothetical.

Which of our pricing tiers is churning fastest, and is it structural or recent?
Connected queries closed-lost and churned accounts by product tier across the last 12 months, compares churn rate by cohort, and flags whether it's accelerating. Answer in ~8 seconds. Without live data: you'd need to pull this manually before you could even ask the question.
We're raising prices next quarter. Which accounts are most likely to push back based on deal history?
Connected pulls discount history, negotiation notes, and contract end dates across your Salesforce accounts. Surfaces the top 20% by churn risk before you start the conversation. Jon's frameworks on price-increase sequencing run against your actual account list — not a template.
Our pipeline coverage looks healthy but close rates are dropping. Where's the break?
Connected calculates pipeline coverage ratio by stage, close rate trends by rep and segment, and average deal velocity over the last 90 days. Cross-references against stage distribution changes. Most pipeline coverage problems are actually late-stage concentration — Connected shows you which one you're dealing with.
What's our net revenue retention by segment, and which segment has the best expansion economics?
Connected queries ARR by account at the start and end of each period, calculates NRR by segment, and ranks segments by expansion rate. Surfaces the segments where your upsell motion is working versus where you're bleeding quietly. NRR by segment is the highest-signal number most teams are flying blind on.
We just ran a discount-heavy quarter to hit the number. What did that actually cost us in ARR?
Connected calculates the difference between contract value at list versus contract value at close across the quarter, computes the ARR impact of average discount depth, and projects the compounding cost at renewal. Jon's view: discounting to hit a number is borrowing from your next quarter at a variable interest rate. Connected shows you the rate.
